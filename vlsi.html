What is VLSI?

Very Large Scale Integration (VLSI) is the process of integrating millions (or billions) of transistors onto a single chip, enabling the development of complex microprocessors, SoCs (System-on-Chip), FPGAs, ASICs, and memory devices. VLSI is the foundation of modern electronics, driving advancements in computing, communications, automotive, healthcare, and more.

VLSI Design Flow

  1. Specification & Architecture: Define requirements, system architecture, and design goals.
  2. RTL Design: Hardware description using Verilog, VHDL, or SystemVerilog.
  3. Functional Verification: Simulation, formal verification, and testbench development.
  4. Synthesis: Convert RTL to gate-level netlist, optimize for area, speed, and power.
  5. Physical Design: Floorplanning, placement, clock tree synthesis, routing, and timing analysis.
  6. DFT & Test: Insert scan chains, BIST, and generate test patterns for manufacturability.
  7. Signoff & Tapeout: Final checks (DRC, LVS, STA) and handoff to fabrication.
  8. Prototyping & Validation: FPGA emulation and post-silicon debug.
